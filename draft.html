Topic & Motivation

Why Now, Why Sydney

Over 4 billion medical imaging studies are performed annually worldwide, yet fewer than 10% are routinely assisted by AI at the point of care. The compute infrastructure to close that gap now exists. What the community still lacks is scientific agreement on how to build on top of it correctly: robustly, safely, and at clinical scale.

Large-scale visual foundation models (FMs) demonstrate remarkable zero-shot transfer across CT, MRI, pathology, and fundus imaging, and some already match or exceed specialist performance on structured tasks. Yet a 2026 cross-sectional study found that frontier multimodal models achieved only ~57.5% accuracy on clinical visual–textual questions, far below expert ophthalmologists, and an international review of 390 clinical AI tools found 98% still in the research phase with only 2% clinically deployed. The bottleneck is not data or architecture alone — it is the absence of compute-aware design principles that make FMs robust, efficient, and deployable across diverse real-world clinical environments.

Three Converging Factors in 2026

Compute platforms are clinical-ready. NVIDIA's medical AI stack — MONAI, Holoscan (FDA-cleared for surgical devices in 2025), Clara, and BioNeMo — represents the first fully integrated compute-to-clinic pipeline for medical imaging.

FMs are powerful but not yet trustworthy. The performance frontier is advancing while the robustness and deployment frontier lags; this gap is the workshop's core scientific problem.

The Sydney moment. NeurIPS 2026 in Sydney is a rare convergence of a flagship global AI conference on Asia-Pacific soil. Australia is home to world-class medical imaging institutions — QUT, CSIRO, NIF, ANSTO, the University of Sydney — and AI-native clinical companies including Harrison.ai. MedCompute 2026 is designed to bring this ecosystem into direct conversation with the global NeurIPS community.

Scope

Topics of Interest

We invite submissions across the following interconnected themes. Full research contributions, position papers, negative results, dataset releases, and infrastructure papers are all explicitly encouraged.

GPU-accelerated image reconstruction — deep unrolling, learned priors, diffusion-based methods for CT, MRI, PET/CT, and ultrasound
Foundation model pre-training for medical imaging — self-supervised objectives, multimodal pre-training, scaling laws, open-source FM releases
Multimodal AI for clinical decision support — vision–language models, cross-modal retrieval, clinical report generation
Synthetic data & generative models — diffusion-based synthesis, privacy preservation, rare pathology simulation, regulatory considerations
Evaluation, benchmarking & robustness — distribution shift, evaluation beyond AUC, dataset curation, prospective vs. retrospective validation
3D and volumetric vision — architectures native to volumetric data, efficient attention, anatomy-aware losses, cross-modality alignment
Interpretability & uncertainty quantification — calibration, conformal prediction, concept-based explanations, human–AI collaboration
Surgical & endoscopic video AI — foundation models for workflow recognition, real-time edge inference, endoscopic benchmarks
Clinical deployment & medical AI systems — federated learning, edge inference, workflow integration, Green AI
Regulatory pathways & governance — FDA, CE Mark under EU AI Act, TGA considerations, post-market surveillance
